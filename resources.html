Implicit Bias Resource Guide

This guide explores implicit bias, intersectionality, and how unconscious stereotypes influence decision-making in education, healthcare, and employment. Below are key resources to help you understand and challenge implicit bias in your personal and professional life.

Understanding Implicit Bias

Implicit bias refers to subconscious associations that shape how we perceive others. These biases can influence hiring, education, and daily interactions.

  • Perception Institute: Implicit Bias Research
  • AAUW - Implicit Bias Research

Implicit Association Test (IAT)

The IAT measures hidden biases. Taking the test can reveal subconscious attitudes and help promote self-awareness.

  • Harvard Implicit Bias Test

Intersectionality & Bias Awareness

Understanding how race, gender, class, and identity overlap helps us recognize complex systems of oppression and privilege.

Student Reflection

Learning about implicit bias made me realize how much of our behavior is influenced by things we don't always notice. Taking the Harvard IAT and reading Robin DiAngelo's work helped me reflect on how bias is embedded in systems—not just individuals. This project made it clear that being aware of bias is only the first step. It’s just as important to take action—whether through inclusive design, ethical decision-making, or challenging injustice in our own spaces.
